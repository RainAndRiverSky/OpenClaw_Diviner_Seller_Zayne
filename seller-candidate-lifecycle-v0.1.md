# Seller Candidate Lifecycle v0.1

## Governance Status

**Artifact Type:** Seller/Zayne Governance Standard  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Initial Governance Baseline  
**Applies To:** Opportunity Candidates transferred to Seller/Zayne  
**Governance Owner:** Seller/Zayne, Commerce Layer of OpenClaw

## 1. Lifecycle Purpose

This document defines the official governance lifecycle for Opportunity Candidates received by Seller/Zayne from Seeker/Xavier or another authorized OpenClaw source.

The lifecycle establishes:

- the recognized governance state of each candidate;
- the conditions under which a candidate may move between states;
- the human authority required for material decisions;
- the circumstances requiring escalation or re-evaluation; and
- the records that must be preserved when a candidate is archived.

A lifecycle state represents governance status only. It does not grant authority to execute, publish, purchase, sell, deploy, commit resources, bind OpenClaw, or act externally.

Each candidate must have one primary lifecycle state at a time. Review conditions, findings, restrictions, and decision qualifications must be recorded without creating ungoverned substitute states.

## 2. Candidate States

The official Seller candidate states are:

1. **Submitted**
2. **Under Review**
3. **Investigating**
4. **Approved**
5. **Rejected**
6. **Escalated**
7. **Archived**

These states are authoritative for lifecycle reporting. More detailed review findings may describe the candidate's condition but must not replace or obscure its official state.

## 3. State Definitions

### 3.1 Submitted

**Submitted** means Seller has received a candidate through an authorized transfer or intake channel, but has not yet accepted the candidate into full evaluation.

While Submitted, Seller must establish:

- candidate identity and reference;
- candidate source;
- date received;
- transfer authority;
- declared purpose and scope;
- available supporting evidence; and
- known omissions, restrictions, or conflicts.

Submission does not imply eligibility, completeness, endorsement, or acceptance for review.

### 3.2 Under Review

**Under Review** means Seller has accepted the candidate for governance evaluation under the Seller review workflow and evaluation standard.

This state may include:

- evidence verification;
- eligibility gate review;
- relationship mapping;
- impact analysis;
- weighted evaluation;
- risk classification;
- decision preparation; and
- required human review.

A candidate remains Under Review until Seller reaches a supported disposition, determines that further investigation is required, or identifies a matter requiring escalation.

### 3.3 Investigating

**Investigating** means the evaluation cannot responsibly proceed to a final disposition because relevant evidence, validation, clarification, dependency determination, or scope resolution remains outstanding.

This state must identify:

- the unresolved matter;
- the evidence or authority needed;
- the responsible evidence or governance owner, when known;
- any review restrictions;
- the effect of the uncertainty on the evaluation; and
- the conditions for returning to Under Review.

Investigating is not an approval and must not be presented as provisional acceptance.

### 3.4 Approved

**Approved** means Seller has completed the required evaluation and issued an approval disposition supported by admissible evidence, satisfied mandatory gates, an acceptable risk classification, and all required human sign-off.

An approval may contain conditions, limits, prohibitions, expiration terms, or review obligations. These qualifications remain part of the approval record.

Approval is limited to the candidate, scope, evidence, assumptions, and authority stated in the decision report. It does not create authority beyond the approved governance scope.

### 3.5 Rejected

**Rejected** means Seller has determined that the candidate does not satisfy intake, eligibility, evidence, risk, governance, or evaluation requirements and cannot proceed in its reviewed form.

The rejection record must state:

- the grounds for rejection;
- the evidence supporting the decision;
- failed gates or disqualifying findings;
- unresolved risks;
- whether reconsideration is permitted; and
- the conditions required for any future re-evaluation.

Rejection findings are persistent. They must remain attached to the candidate record unless formally resolved or superseded through documented re-evaluation.

### 3.6 Escalated

**Escalated** means Seller has identified a matter that exceeds Seller's delegated authority, presents a material governance conflict, or requires a higher or specialized authority to determine whether and how review may continue.

The escalation record must identify:

- the issue requiring authority;
- the reason Seller cannot resolve it independently;
- the affected evidence, gate, risk, or decision;
- the authority or governance layer receiving the escalation;
- any permitted interim review activity; and
- the decision required to resume or close the lifecycle.

Escalation does not imply approval or rejection.

### 3.7 Archived

**Archived** means active Seller review and decision activity has ended and the candidate's governance record has been preserved.

A candidate may be Archived after:

- completion or expiration of an approval;
- final rejection;
- resolved escalation with no further review;
- authorized withdrawal;
- loss of relevance;
- supersession by a separately referenced candidate;
- expiration without authorized revalidation; or
- another documented closure decision.

Archival does not erase findings, reverse a decision, or permit the record to be reused without its history and qualifications.

## 4. Allowed Transitions

Every transition must have a recorded reason, date, responsible authority, and supporting governance record.

| Current State | Allowed Next State | Governing Condition |
|---|---|---|
| Submitted | Under Review | Intake requirements are sufficiently satisfied and Seller accepts the candidate for evaluation. |
| Submitted | Investigating | Material intake evidence or clarification is missing but may reasonably be obtained. |
| Submitted | Rejected | A disqualifying intake defect, prohibited condition, or non-remediable eligibility failure is established. |
| Submitted | Escalated | Intake presents an authority conflict, material governance dispute, or high-consequence issue outside Seller authority. |
| Submitted | Archived | The submission is withdrawn, duplicated, superseded, expired, or closed before acceptance, with the reason preserved. |
| Under Review | Investigating | Missing, stale, conflicting, uncertain, or dependency-controlled evidence prevents a supported decision. |
| Under Review | Approved | Required evidence, mandatory gates, evaluation, risk review, and sign-off support approval. |
| Under Review | Rejected | The completed review supports rejection. |
| Under Review | Escalated | A decision or unresolved matter exceeds Seller authority or requires higher review. |
| Under Review | Archived | The candidate is withdrawn, superseded, or otherwise formally closed before disposition. |
| Investigating | Under Review | Required evidence, clarification, validation, or dependency determination becomes available and review can resume. |
| Investigating | Rejected | Investigation confirms a disqualifying condition or establishes that required evidence cannot support continued review. |
| Investigating | Escalated | The unresolved matter requires authority beyond Seller or presents a material governance conflict. |
| Investigating | Archived | Investigation is closed due to withdrawal, expiration, supersession, or authorized discontinuation. |
| Approved | Under Review | A re-evaluation condition materially affects the basis, scope, validity, or risk of approval. |
| Approved | Escalated | A material post-approval issue requires higher authority or immediate governance intervention. |
| Approved | Archived | The approved lifecycle is complete, expired, withdrawn, superseded, or otherwise formally closed. |
| Rejected | Under Review | Authorized re-evaluation is supported by materially new evidence or a documented resolution of rejection findings. |
| Rejected | Escalated | Reconsideration depends on an authority decision or unresolved governance dispute. |
| Rejected | Archived | The rejection is final for the reviewed candidate and no active escalation or re-evaluation remains. |
| Escalated | Under Review | The responsible authority resolves the escalation and authorizes Seller to resume evaluation. |
| Escalated | Investigating | The responsible authority requires additional evidence, clarification, or narrowed scope. |
| Escalated | Rejected | The responsible authority confirms that the candidate must not proceed. |
| Escalated | Archived | The escalation is closed without further review or the candidate is withdrawn, expired, or superseded. |
| Archived | Under Review | Exceptional reopening is authorized and the original record remains preserved with a new review reference. |

Transitions not listed above are prohibited.

An Archived candidate should ordinarily remain closed. A materially changed proposal should enter as a new Submitted candidate linked to the archived record rather than replacing it.

## 5. Human Authority Requirements

Human review and sign-off are required before:

- a candidate enters Approved;
- a material escalation is resolved;
- a rejected candidate is reopened;
- an archived candidate is reopened;
- a persistent rejection finding is declared resolved or superseded;
- a mandatory gate failure is disputed or reconsidered;
- a high or critical risk is accepted;
- a decision materially affects rights, privacy, safety, compliance, financial exposure, vulnerable users, or external obligations; or
- an exception to the normal lifecycle is authorized.

Human authority must be appropriate to the decision and must not be assumed from participation, silence, prior approval, or organizational proximity.

The sign-off record must identify:

- reviewer name or accountable authority;
- role and scope of authority;
- candidate reference and review identifier;
- lifecycle transition authorized;
- evidence and decision report reviewed;
- conditions, restrictions, unresolved matters, and expiration terms;
- review date; and
- explicit approval, rejection, escalation resolution, reopening, or closure decision.

Human review cannot:

- convert missing or uncertain evidence into verified fact;
- erase adverse findings without documented resolution;
- validate stale, inadmissible, or materially misleading evidence;
- waive applicable law, policy, rights, or user agency;
- transfer authority that the reviewer does not possess; or
- authorize activity beyond the stated candidate scope.

## 6. Escalation Conditions

A candidate must enter Escalated when:

- the required decision exceeds Seller's delegated authority;
- governance artifacts provide conflicting requirements that Seller cannot reconcile;
- evidence ownership or validation authority is disputed;
- a mandatory gate outcome is contested by an authority necessary to proceed;
- unresolved high or critical risk requires acceptance by a higher authority;
- the candidate may materially affect legal, regulatory, privacy, safety, platform, financial, contractual, or rights obligations;
- the candidate creates or expands external authority, commitments, or irreversible consequences;
- a conflict of interest compromises independent review;
- pressure to proceed would bypass required evidence, gates, or human review;
- persistent rejection findings are challenged without sufficient Seller authority to resolve them; or
- disagreement among responsible governance layers prevents a supported disposition.

An escalation must preserve the current evidence status and unresolved findings. It must not be used to imply that the candidate has passed review.

## 7. Re-Evaluation Conditions

Re-evaluation is required when a material change may affect the validity of a prior review or decision, including:

- evidence expires, becomes stale, is corrected, withdrawn, or discredited;
- new counterevidence or material evidence becomes available;
- a verified fact changes;
- an inferred relationship becomes verified, disproved, or materially revised;
- a new direct, indirect, or transitive impact is identified;
- candidate scope, audience, use, claims, terms, obligations, or dependencies change;
- a responsible evidence owner or governance layer changes its determination;
- new legal, compliance, rights, privacy, safety, platform, operational, financial, or governance concerns arise;
- approval conditions are not satisfied or are breached;
- the authority supporting the decision changes or expires;
- a persistent rejection finding is claimed to have been resolved; or
- an escalation decision authorizes renewed review.

Re-evaluation must:

- preserve the previous decision and evidence record;
- identify the triggering change;
- distinguish new evidence from previously reviewed evidence;
- reassess affected gates, scores, risks, relationships, and impacts;
- retain unresolved rejection findings;
- issue a new review identifier and review timestamp; and
- obtain renewed human sign-off when required.

A prior score, approval, or sign-off does not automatically survive material change.

## 8. Archive Conditions

Archival may occur only when active governance work has ended or has been formally discontinued.

Before transition to Archived:

- the closure reason must be recorded;
- the final active state and disposition must be preserved;
- required human sign-off must be complete;
- active escalations must be resolved or expressly closed;
- unresolved findings and risks must remain visible;
- superseding or related candidate references must be recorded;
- any expiration, withdrawal, or discontinuation must be dated; and
- the governance record must be sufficiently complete for later audit.

The archived record must preserve, as applicable:

- candidate identity and reference;
- source and transfer information;
- intake record;
- evidence inventory and evidence classifications;
- freshness determinations;
- verified facts, inferred relationships, and uncertain evidence;
- missing evidence register;
- relationship and impact assessments;
- mandatory gate results;
- weighted scores and rationales;
- risk classifications;
- critical and persistent rejection findings;
- decision reports;
- human reviews and sign-offs;
- escalation records and authority determinations;
- re-evaluation history;
- transition history; and
- archive date and archive authority.

Archival must not conceal disagreement, remove adverse evidence, overwrite prior decisions, or imply that an unresolved matter was resolved.

## 9. Lifecycle Diagram

The primary lifecycle path is:

**Submitted → Under Review → Approved or Rejected → Archived**

Evidence or dependency gaps may create the following review cycle:

**Submitted or Under Review → Investigating → Under Review**

Authority or material governance issues may create the following escalation path:

**Submitted, Under Review, Investigating, Approved, or Rejected → Escalated**

After an authority determination:

**Escalated → Under Review, Investigating, Rejected, or Archived**

Material changes may reopen an active decision:

**Approved or Rejected → Under Review**

Exceptional reopening of a closed record requires explicit authority:

**Archived → Under Review**

Where the candidate has materially changed, the preferred lifecycle is:

**Archived Candidate → Linked New Candidate → Submitted**

## 10. Governance Principle

The Seller candidate lifecycle must preserve a clear distinction between submission, evaluation, investigation, decision, escalation, and closure. No candidate may advance through implication, urgency, incomplete evidence, or unrecorded authority.

Every state and transition must remain attributable, reviewable, and auditable throughout the life of the candidate record.
