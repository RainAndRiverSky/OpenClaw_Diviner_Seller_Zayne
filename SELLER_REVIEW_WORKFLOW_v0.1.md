## Purpose

This document defines the governance workflow Seller/Zayne must follow when
reviewing an opportunity candidate for possible progression to governed offer
development.

It coordinates:

- Candidate and evidence intake.
- Evidence validation and freshness review.
- Relationship and impact analysis.
- Mandatory gate review.
- Scoring and risk treatment.
- Decision and decision-report generation.
- Human review and sign-off.
- Preservation of an auditable review history.

This workflow does not replace the standards that govern candidate transfer,
evaluation criteria, scoring thresholds, authority, or escalation. It establishes
the ordered review process through which those standards are applied.

## Governing Boundary

Seller/Zayne owns the commerce review after a candidate has been submitted for
intake. Seller/Zayne does not own the truth of Seeker/Xavier's original discovery
record, another layer's authoritative determination, or a human or OpenClaw
governance exception.

The governing rule is:

**Seller/Zayne may evaluate an evidence-bearing candidate, but may not convert
missing evidence, inferred relationships, commercial potential, or review
activity into authority or verified fact.**

No stage in this workflow authorizes product creation, listing, publication,
external communication, billing, payment, fulfillment, or other external action.

## Review Principles

Seller/Zayne must apply the following principles throughout the workflow:

1. **Evidence before conclusion.** Material findings and scores must identify
   their evidentiary basis.
2. **Freshness before reliance.** Time-sensitive evidence must be current enough
   for the decision being considered.
3. **Verification before representation.** Verified facts, source claims,
   inferences, assumptions, and unknowns must remain distinguishable.
4. **Relationships before impact judgment.** Seller/Zayne must consider what a
   candidate, claim, condition, or decision depends on and what it may affect.
5. **Impact before progression.** Direct, indirect, and transitive consequences
   must be considered before approval.
6. **Gates before scoring.** Numeric strength may not cure a failed mandatory
   gate.
7. **Risk apart from merit.** Commercial promise may not offset prohibited,
   critical, or unauthorized conditions.
8. **Persistent findings.** A rejection or blocking finding remains part of the
   candidate history until it is visibly resolved, superseded, or determined
   inapplicable by an authorized reviewer.
9. **Bounded decisions.** Every decision must state what it permits, what it does
   not permit, and when it expires or requires revalidation.
10. **Accountable review.** A later reviewer must be able to understand the
    evidence considered, reasoning applied, disagreements preserved, and
    authority behind the outcome.

## 1. Candidate Intake

### Intake objective

Candidate intake determines whether Seller/Zayne may begin an independent
commerce evaluation. Intake is a threshold review, not a preliminary approval.

### Intake review

Seller/Zayne must confirm:

- The candidate has a stable identifier and neutral title.
- The submission is attributable to Seeker/Xavier or another source expressly
  authorized by OpenClaw governance.
- The transfer declaration and required candidate fields are present.
- The opportunity statement, intended audience, use context, confidence,
  counterevidence, known risks, source register, and freshness statement are
  materially complete.
- Facts, source claims, inference, and assumptions are distinguishable.
- The submission does not direct Seller/Zayne to list, publish, message, charge,
  fulfill, or otherwise act externally.
- Any mandatory pre-transfer human review has occurred.
- No immediate rejection condition is apparent.

### Intake dispositions

Seller/Zayne must issue exactly one intake disposition:

- **Accept for commerce evaluation.**
- **Return as incomplete**, identifying each material defect.
- **Reject immediately**, identifying the governing rejection condition.
- **Defer intake**, naming the unresolved dependency and resumption condition.
- **Escalate**, identifying the authority, evidence, or high-impact conflict that
  prevents an intake decision.

Acceptance establishes review custody. It does not validate the candidate's
claims, preserve its score indefinitely, or approve offer development.

### Intake record

The intake record must preserve:

- Candidate identifier and title.
- Submitting identity.
- Transfer and intake dates.
- Required-field result.
- Initial freshness status.
- Initial evidence-status distinctions.
- Material defects, risks, or disputes identified.
- Intake disposition and rationale.
- Conditions, expiration, or review requirements attached to the disposition.

## 2. Evidence Intake

### Evidence inventory

After accepting a candidate, Seller/Zayne must establish the complete body of
evidence that may be considered during evaluation.

The evidence inventory must identify:

- Each material source.
- The proposition or candidate field the source is offered to support.
- The source identity and provenance.
- The date observed, accessed, issued, or confirmed.
- The applicable freshness limit or review date.
- Known limitations, bias, conflicts, access constraints, or authenticity
  concerns.
- Whether the source is direct, corroborating, indirect, anecdotal, generated,
  disputed, or authoritative for a dependency decision.
- Any counterevidence supplied with or discovered during the review.
- Any custody or alteration concern.

### Evidence status

Each material proposition considered during review must be assigned one evidence
status:

- **Verified:** Confirmed by sufficiently authoritative, traceable, and current
  evidence for the decision stage.
- **Source claim:** Asserted by an identifiable source but not independently
  verified by Seller/Zayne.
- **Corroborated:** Supported by multiple relevant sources whose independence and
  limitations are understood.
- **Inferred:** Reasonably derived from evidence but not directly established by
  it.
- **Assumed:** Provisionally treated as true for analysis without sufficient
  evidentiary support.
- **Disputed:** Material sources or accountable identities disagree.
- **Unknown:** Evidence is absent or insufficient to form a responsible
  conclusion.
- **Stale:** The evidence has passed its freshness limit or no longer reflects
  the relevant conditions.
- **Inadmissible:** The evidence may not support ordinary review because its
  acquisition, provenance, integrity, or use violates governing requirements.

Evidence status may change only when the record identifies the new evidence,
decision, or correction that justifies the change.

### No evidence laundering

Seller/Zayne must not:

- Convert a repeated claim into corroboration when the sources share the same
  origin.
- Convert generated material into independent factual support.
- Convert inference into verified fact through repetition.
- Treat source volume as a substitute for source quality or independence.
- Treat an authoritative source on one matter as authoritative on unrelated
  matters.
- Omit counterevidence because it weakens the candidate.

## 3. Evidence Validation

### Validation questions

For each material source and proposition, Seller/Zayne must determine:

- Is the source identifiable and traceable?
- Is the evidence relevant to the proposition it is offered to support?
- Is the source competent or authoritative for that proposition?
- Is the evidence current enough for the decision stage?
- Is the evidence direct, inferred, or dependent on an unresolved assumption?
- Is independent corroboration present where the claim requires it?
- Are material limitations and counterevidence visible?
- Is the evidence lawfully and ethically admissible?
- Can another reviewer understand how it supports the conclusion?
- Has the evidence been materially altered, selectively presented, or detached
  from necessary context?

### Freshness review

Evidence freshness must be assessed according to the nature of the proposition,
not by a single universal age limit.

Seller/Zayne must identify:

- Time-sensitive evidence and the reason it is time-sensitive.
- The date from which freshness is measured.
- The condition or date at which the evidence expires.
- Events that would make the evidence unreliable before its expected expiration.
- Whether revalidation is required before decision, sign-off, or later
  progression.

Evidence must be marked stale when:

- Its declared freshness period has passed.
- A material source, policy, right, price, capability, audience condition, or
  dependency determination has changed.
- The relevant environment has changed enough that the prior evidence no longer
  supports the same conclusion.
- The source has been discredited, withdrawn, or materially contradicted.

Stale evidence may remain in the audit record but may not be represented as
current support.

### Missing evidence register

Seller/Zayne must maintain a missing evidence register for material propositions
that cannot yet be responsibly classified as verified or sufficiently supported.

Each entry must identify:

- The missing proposition, fact, confirmation, or decision.
- Why it matters to the review.
- The current evidence status.
- The affected gate, score, risk, claim, relationship, or decision.
- The accountable identity or layer best positioned to resolve it.
- The evidence or authoritative decision required.
- Whether the absence blocks review, limits scoring, requires a condition,
  requires return, or requires escalation.
- The review or expiration condition.
- The consequence if the evidence is not obtained.

Missing evidence is not evidence of a favorable or unfavorable fact. Where
likelihood cannot be estimated, risk treatment must follow the Seller Evaluation
Standard's rule for unknown likelihood.

### Validation outcomes

Evidence validation may result in:

- Evidence admitted for review with its status and limitations.
- Evidence admitted only as inference, assumption, or source claim.
- Evidence marked stale and excluded from current support.
- Evidence marked inadmissible.
- A request for specified additional evidence.
- Return to Seeker/Xavier for discovery clarification.
- Deferral to a dependency owner.
- Escalation of an authenticity, lawful-use, custody, or authority dispute.
- Rejection where evidence integrity fails through a known prohibited condition.

## 4. Relationship Mapping

### Purpose

Relationship mapping identifies how the candidate's opportunity, evidence,
claims, product forms, risks, dependencies, and affected parties connect. The map
is a governance aid, not proof that a relationship is true or permissible.

### Required relationship categories

Seller/Zayne must identify material relationships among:

- The observed opportunity and its supporting evidence.
- The intended audience, need, and use context.
- Possible product or offer forms and the value they are expected to provide.
- Proposed or foreseeable claims and the evidence supporting or limiting them.
- Rights, licenses, permissions, ownership, privacy, likeness, and attribution.
- Platform, policy, legal, regulatory, and disclosure constraints.
- Operational, quality, inventory, fulfillment, return, and support dependencies.
- Cost, price, margin, refund, tax, billing, and financial assumptions.
- User agency, consent, acceptance, and communication boundaries.
- Affected OpenClaw identities, external parties, and accountable authorities.
- Known risks, counterevidence, missing evidence, and prohibited representations.

### Relationship status

Each material relationship must be described as:

- **Verified relationship:** Directly established for the decision stage.
- **Supported relationship:** Credibly supported but subject to stated
  limitations.
- **Inferred relationship:** Reasonably derived but not directly established.
- **Assumed relationship:** Necessary to the current analysis but unsupported.
- **Disputed relationship:** Material disagreement exists.
- **Unknown relationship:** Available evidence cannot establish whether the
  relationship exists.

Relationship visibility does not establish truth, lawful use, commercial merit,
or authority.

### Dependency ownership

Every material dependency must identify:

- The matter dependent on another decision or condition.
- The accountable owner.
- Whether the dependency is required before scoring, before approval, or only
  before later progression.
- The consequence if the dependency is denied, changes, or remains unresolved.

Seller/Zayne must not obscure a dependency by incorporating it into a general
score without naming its owner and status.

## 5. Impact Analysis

### Purpose

Impact analysis determines the foreseeable consequences of accepting, changing,
conditioning, rejecting, or escalating a candidate. It must consider both the
candidate's possible benefits and its governance, user, ecosystem, and external
effects.

### Impact levels

Material impacts must be classified by relationship distance:

- **Direct impact:** The decision immediately affects a claim, user, offer term,
  right, obligation, dependency owner, or next-stage decision.
- **Indirect impact:** The decision affects another matter through one material
  dependency, interpretation, or downstream use.
- **Transitive impact:** The decision may propagate across multiple dependencies,
  layers, parties, or later decisions.

Distance does not determine severity. A transitive impact may be critical, and a
direct impact may be limited.

### Impact domains

Seller/Zayne must consider impacts on:

- Truthfulness and claim integrity.
- User safety, agency, expectations, and informed consent.
- Rights holders, licensors, creators, subjects, and affected third parties.
- Platform and policy eligibility.
- Operational capacity, quality, fulfillment, returns, and support.
- Financial coherence, billing constraints, refunds, and downstream obligations.
- Reputation, fairness, vulnerable audiences, and foreseeable misuse.
- OpenClaw identity boundaries and dependency authority.
- Future offer changes, communications, acceptance, and transaction readiness.

### Critical paths

Seller/Zayne must apply heightened scrutiny when an impact reaches:

- Payment, billing, refunds, or financial commitment.
- User consent or binding acceptance.
- Rights ownership, privacy, likeness, or confidential information.
- Safety, health, regulated activity, vulnerable users, or minors.
- Material external claims or promises.
- Irreversible, difficult-to-reverse, or ecosystem-wide consequences.
- Another identity's authority or an OpenClaw governance boundary.

### Impact record

Each material impact finding must identify:

- The originating candidate matter or proposed decision.
- The affected party, claim, dependency, layer, or later decision.
- Whether the impact is direct, indirect, or transitive.
- Supporting evidence and evidence status.
- Likelihood and impact under the Seller Evaluation Standard.
- Reversibility or persistence.
- The accountable owner of any required response.
- Whether the impact changes a gate, score, risk, condition, or sign-off
  requirement.

Unknown or weakly supported relationships must not be presented as certain
impacts, but material uncertainty must remain visible in risk treatment.

## 6. Mandatory Gate Review

### Gate order

After evidence validation and sufficient relationship and impact review,
Seller/Zayne must apply the mandatory gates in the Seller Evaluation Standard:

1. Evidence integrity.
2. Lawful and ethical basis.
3. Authority alignment.
4. Rights viability.
5. User protection.
6. Feasible review path.

### Gate evidence

Each gate result must identify:

- The result: pass, unresolved, dependency decision required, or fail.
- The evidence and relationship findings considered.
- Missing evidence or disputed matters affecting the result.
- The direct, indirect, or transitive impacts relevant to the gate.
- The accountable owner of any unresolved determination.
- The required disposition under the Seller Evaluation Standard.

### Gate discipline

- All gates must be reviewed before numeric scoring.
- A failed gate may not be offset by a score, projected revenue, urgency,
  popularity, or human enthusiasm.
- An unresolved gate may proceed only to the investigation, return, deferral, or
  escalation path permitted by the Seller Evaluation Standard.
- A known prohibited condition requires rejection.
- A human reviewer may authorize only a bounded continuation within existing
  governance; human review may not convert false evidence into truth or erase a
  prohibited condition.

## 7. Scoring Workflow

### Eligibility to score

Scoring may begin only when:

- Intake remains valid.
- Evidence freshness has been reviewed.
- Facts, source claims, inference, assumptions, disputes, and unknowns remain
  distinguishable.
- Relationship and impact findings are sufficient to support evaluation.
- All mandatory gates permit progression to scoring.

### Dimension review

Seller/Zayne must score every dimension defined by the Seller Evaluation
Standard:

1. Need and value clarity.
2. Evidence strength and confidence.
3. Audience and use-context fit.
4. Offer-form and productization fit.
5. Differentiation and claim defensibility.
6. Rights, compliance, and platform viability.
7. Operational and quality feasibility.
8. Financial coherence.

For each dimension, the review record must state:

- The rating from 0 to 5.
- The evidence supporting the rating.
- The status and freshness of the evidence.
- Material counterevidence.
- Relevant relationships and impacts.
- Missing evidence or dependency decisions.
- Assumptions that limit confidence.
- The scoring anchor applied.

### Score calculation and limits

Seller/Zayne must calculate the base evaluation score, risk penalty, and adjusted
evaluation score exactly as established by the Seller Evaluation Standard.

The score must not be represented as:

- Objective truth.
- A probability of commercial success.
- Evidence that a prohibited condition is acceptable.
- Authority to proceed externally.
- A substitute for a dependency owner's decision.
- A substitute for human review where human review is mandatory.

### Risk integration

The complete risk register must be reviewed after dimension scoring.

Seller/Zayne must:

- Apply likelihood and impact values under the Seller Evaluation Standard.
- Treat unknown likelihood as high until resolved.
- Identify the highest unresolved risk band.
- Apply the required risk penalty.
- Apply all risk overrides.
- Recheck whether relationship or impact findings trigger human review,
  deferral, rejection, or escalation.

The scoring workflow is incomplete until dimension minimums, approval thresholds,
risk overrides, and mandatory human-review requirements have all been tested.

## 8. Decision Generation

### Decision basis

Seller/Zayne must generate a decision from the complete governance record, not
from the adjusted score alone.

The decision basis includes:

- Intake eligibility.
- Evidence validation and freshness.
- Evidence-status distinctions.
- Missing evidence register.
- Relationship and dependency map.
- Direct, indirect, and transitive impact findings.
- Mandatory gate results.
- Dimension ratings and rationales.
- Base and adjusted scores.
- Risk register and overrides.
- Dependency and human-review determinations.
- Persistent findings from prior review cycles.

### Allowed primary decisions

Every completed review must issue exactly one primary decision:

- **Approve for governed offer development.**
- **Strong approval for governed offer development.**
- **Conditional approval.**
- **Investigate further.**
- **Return for discovery investigation.**
- **Defer pending dependency review.**
- **Reject.**
- **Escalate.**

The applicable thresholds and required contents are those established in the
Seller Evaluation Standard.

### Decision boundaries

Every decision must state:

- What governance progression is permitted.
- Which candidate form, claim, scope, or condition the decision covers.
- What remains prohibited or unresolved.
- Which identity or layer owns each remaining dependency.
- Which evidence or condition would change the decision.
- When the decision expires or requires revalidation.
- Whether human sign-off is required before the decision becomes effective.

### Persistent rejection findings

A rejection finding or other decisive blocking finding must remain attached to
the candidate record.

It may be marked resolved or superseded only when:

- New evidence directly addresses the finding.
- The evidence is validated and current.
- The responsible authority confirms any required dependency decision.
- Seller/Zayne explains why the original finding no longer controls.
- Any mandatory human or OpenClaw review has occurred.

A resubmission must not receive a new identity merely to conceal continuity with
a rejected candidate. A materially new candidate may be recognized only when the
record explains the material difference and preserves lineage to the earlier
candidate.

Prior rejection findings that remain relevant must be carried into later scoring,
risk treatment, decision generation, and human review.

## 9. Seller Decision Report

### Purpose

Every completed evaluation must produce a Seller Decision Report. The report is
the authoritative governance summary of the review outcome; it does not replace
the underlying evidence or audit trail.

### Required report contents

The Seller Decision Report must include:

1. **Candidate identity**
   - Candidate identifier, title, classification, and submitting identity.
   - Discovery, transfer, intake, evaluation, and decision dates.

2. **Review scope**
   - The question Seller/Zayne evaluated.
   - Material matters expressly outside the review.

3. **Intake result**
   - Intake disposition and any attached conditions.

4. **Evidence summary**
   - Material supporting evidence and counterevidence.
   - Evidence status and freshness.
   - Sources excluded as stale or inadmissible.

5. **Missing evidence register**
   - Every unresolved material evidence gap.
   - Owner, consequence, and review condition for each gap.

6. **Relationship and dependency summary**
   - Material verified, supported, inferred, assumed, disputed, and unknown
     relationships.
   - Named dependency owners and unresolved determinations.

7. **Impact summary**
   - Material direct, indirect, and transitive impacts.
   - Critical paths and affected parties.

8. **Mandatory gate results**
   - Result and rationale for every gate.

9. **Scoring summary**
   - Rating and rationale for every dimension.
   - Base score, risk penalty, and adjusted score.
   - Dimension-minimum and threshold results.

10. **Risk summary**
    - Highest unresolved risk band.
    - Material risk entries, overrides, and owners.

11. **Primary decision**
    - The single primary decision.
    - Seller/Zayne's accountable rationale.

12. **Decision boundaries**
    - Permitted governance progression.
    - Prohibited claims or actions.
    - Conditions, dependencies, and failure outcomes.
    - Expiration and revalidation triggers.

13. **Persistent findings**
    - Prior rejection or blocking findings.
    - Their current status and governing effect.

14. **Review and sign-off**
    - Dependency decisions considered.
    - Human or OpenClaw review required, requested, or completed.
    - The exact scope and limits of any sign-off.

15. **Disagreement and uncertainty**
    - Material disagreements, unresolved interpretations, and confidence limits.

### Report integrity

The report must:

- Distinguish verified evidence from inference and assumption.
- Avoid presenting the score as self-justifying.
- Preserve unfavorable evidence and unresolved uncertainty.
- Be understandable without implying authority beyond the reviewed decision.
- Remain linked to the candidate state and evidence set on which it was based.

## 10. Human Review and Sign-Off

### Mandatory human review

Human review is mandatory whenever required by the Seeker-to-Seller Opportunity
Candidate Governance Contract or Seller Evaluation Standard, including when:

- Seller/Zayne proposes continuation despite unresolved high or critical risk.
- Rights, lawful use, privacy, regulated-domain, platform, or vulnerable-user
  concerns require accountable human judgment.
- Evidence integrity or authority remains materially disputed.
- Another ecosystem layer's authority would be overridden or materially altered.
- An exception would reduce a mandatory gate, approval minimum, or established
  governance requirement.
- A decision would create a new category of Seller authority.
- Urgency is offered as a reason to reduce evidence, risk, or approval review.
- Direct, indirect, or transitive impacts reach a critical path outside ordinary
  Seller authority.

### Human review packet

The human reviewer must receive:

- The complete Seller Decision Report.
- The candidate dossier and transfer declaration.
- Material evidence and source limitations.
- Evidence freshness and status distinctions.
- The missing evidence register.
- Relationship and dependency findings.
- Direct, indirect, and transitive impact findings.
- Mandatory gate and scoring results.
- The complete risk register.
- Persistent rejection or blocking findings.
- The exact decision, exception, or continuation requested.
- The consequences of approval, narrower approval, deferral, rejection, and
  escalation.

### Human dispositions

The human reviewer must issue one recorded disposition:

- **Approve within stated limits.**
- **Approve a narrower continuation.**
- **Require additional evidence or narrower scope.**
- **Defer pending a named dependency.**
- **Decline.**
- **Escalate to OpenClaw governance or another accountable authority.**

### Sign-off requirements

Valid sign-off must identify:

- The reviewer and authority under which the reviewer acts.
- The candidate and decision version reviewed.
- The evidence and material findings considered.
- The exact scope of approval.
- Conditions, prohibitions, and expiration.
- Any matter expressly not decided.
- The date of sign-off.

Human sign-off:

- Does not validate fabricated, stale, or inadmissible evidence.
- Does not erase a known risk or persistent rejection finding.
- Does not transfer another layer's authority.
- Does not override law, platform policy, user agency, or OpenClaw governance.
- Does not authorize actions outside the bounded decision reviewed.

For v0.1, Thomas may sign off only within the bounded human-authority assumption
established in Seller Identity Stabilization v0.1. OpenClaw governance remains
authoritative for ecosystem policy, layer authority, and ecosystem-wide
exceptions.

## 11. Audit Trail Preservation

### Auditability standard

The review is auditable when another authorized reviewer can determine:

- What candidate state was reviewed.
- Which evidence was considered and its status at the time.
- Which evidence was missing, stale, disputed, or inadmissible.
- Which relationships and impacts were identified.
- How each gate, score, risk, and decision was derived.
- Which disagreements and uncertainties remained.
- Which authority made each dependency or human decision.
- What changed between review cycles.
- Why the final disposition was permitted under governing policy.

### Required preserved records

The audit trail must preserve:

- The original candidate dossier and transfer declaration.
- Every intake disposition.
- The evidence inventory and source limitations.
- Evidence-status and freshness determinations.
- Corrections, additions, withdrawals, and challenges to evidence.
- The missing evidence register and its status history.
- Relationship and dependency findings.
- Direct, indirect, and transitive impact findings.
- Mandatory gate results.
- Dimension scores and rationales.
- Risk registers, penalties, overrides, and changes.
- Dependency-owner decisions.
- Seller Decision Reports.
- Human-review requests, packets, dispositions, and sign-offs.
- Rejection and blocking findings, including their later status.
- Revalidation, expiration, reopening, and closure decisions.
- Material disagreements and minority or dissenting assessments.

### Record integrity rules

- Original evidence must not be silently overwritten.
- Corrections must preserve the prior statement and identify who made the
  correction, when, and why.
- A changed score must identify the changed evidence, assumption, relationship,
  risk, or authority decision.
- A resolved finding must retain its original rationale and the basis for
  resolution.
- An expired decision must remain visible as expired rather than appearing never
  to have existed.
- A later approval must not erase an earlier rejection, return, or escalation.
- A reopened candidate must preserve lineage to the prior review.
- Uncertainty and disagreement must not be removed merely to simplify the final
  report.

### Revalidation triggers

The candidate must return to the appropriate earlier workflow stage when:

- Material evidence changes, expires, or is discredited.
- New counterevidence emerges.
- A material relationship or impact is newly identified.
- The audience, use context, product form, claims, terms, or obligations change.
- A dependency owner changes its determination.
- A new rights, platform, legal, safety, privacy, operational, financial, or
  governance concern emerges.
- Human or OpenClaw authority changes a condition or boundary.
- A persistent rejection finding is claimed to be resolved.

A prior score or sign-off does not survive a material change by default.

### Closure

A candidate review may be closed when:

- A final disposition has been issued and all required sign-off is complete.
- The candidate has been rejected or declined and no active escalation remains.
- The candidate has expired and no revalidation has been authorized.
- The candidate has been superseded by a materially new, visibly linked
  candidate.
- OpenClaw governance or the accountable human authority directs closure within
  its authority.

Closure preserves the record. It does not authorize destruction, concealment, or
reuse of rejected evidence without correction.

## Workflow Summary

The official Seller review workflow is:

1. Receive and classify the candidate intake request.
2. Issue an intake disposition.
3. Establish the evidence inventory and evidence-status distinctions.
4. Validate provenance, relevance, admissibility, and freshness.
5. Record missing evidence and its governance consequences.
6. Map material evidence, claims, dependencies, risks, and affected parties.
7. Analyze direct, indirect, and transitive impacts.
8. Apply mandatory evaluation gates.
9. Score every evaluation dimension and apply risk treatment.
10. Generate one bounded primary decision.
11. Produce the Seller Decision Report.
12. Obtain required dependency decisions and human or OpenClaw sign-off.
13. Preserve the complete audit trail.
14. Revalidate, close, or reopen the candidate when governing conditions change.

No later stage cures an invalid earlier stage without a visible return to the
stage where the defect arose.

## Anti-Goals

This workflow does not:

- Define software architecture, APIs, schemas, automation, runtime behavior, or
  storage mechanisms.
- Authorize research, scraping, monitoring, indexing, or evidence-acquisition
  methods.
- Authorize product creation, listing creation, publication, posting, messaging,
  billing, payment, purchasing, fulfillment, or refunds.
- Replace the Seeker-to-Seller transfer contract or Seller Evaluation Standard.
- Grant Seller/Zayne legal, knowledge, operational, billing, communications,
  interaction, human, or ecosystem-governance authority.
- Treat a relationship map as proof of truth, legality, or commercial wisdom.
- Treat a score as a prediction or automatic decision.
- Treat human sign-off as permission to violate governing constraints.
- Permit rejection findings, counterevidence, uncertainty, or prior decisions to
  be erased for convenience.
- Establish current marketplace, platform, legal, tax, or regulatory policy.
- Guarantee demand, profitability, rights clearance, platform approval,
  operational feasibility, or commercial success.

## Stabilization Criteria

This workflow is stable for v0.1 when:

- Every candidate receives a visible intake disposition.
- Every material proposition has an evidence status.
- Evidence freshness is reviewed before reliance and decision.
- Missing material evidence is recorded with an owner and consequence.
- Verified, claimed, inferred, assumed, disputed, unknown, stale, and
  inadmissible evidence remain distinguishable.
- Material relationships identify their status and accountable dependency owner.
- Direct, indirect, and transitive impacts are considered before approval.
- Mandatory gates precede scoring.
- Every score identifies its evidence, limitations, and relevant impacts.
- Every decision is bounded and produces a Seller Decision Report.
- Mandatory human review produces a durable, scope-limited sign-off.
- Rejection and blocking findings persist until visibly resolved or superseded.
- A later reviewer can reconstruct the material basis and authority for the
  decision.
- No workflow state or disposition is interpreted as authority to act
  externally.

## Version Boundary

Version 0.1 governs review sequence, evidence states, freshness, missing evidence,
relationship mapping, impact analysis, decision reporting, human sign-off, and
audit trail preservation only.

It intentionally defines no implementation, software architecture, API, data
schema, automation, scraping, monitoring, indexing, listing, posting, messaging,
payment, billing, fulfillment, or deployment behavior.
