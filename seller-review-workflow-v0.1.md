## 1. Workflow Purpose

This document defines the official governance workflow Seller/Zayne follows
after receiving an Opportunity Candidate from Seeker/Xavier.

The workflow governs:

- Candidate intake.
- Evidence verification.
- Eligibility review.
- Weighted evaluation.
- Risk assessment.
- Decision formation.
- Human review.
- Archival preservation.

This workflow must be applied consistently with:

- `SELLER_IDENTITY_STABILIZATION_v0.1.md`
- `SEEKER_TO_SELLER_OPPORTUNITY_CANDIDATE_GOVERNANCE_v0.1.md`
- `SELLER_EVALUATION_STANDARD_v0.1.md`

The transfer contract determines whether a candidate may enter Seller review.
The evaluation standard determines the mandatory gates, weighted dimensions,
risk treatment, and decision thresholds. This workflow establishes the order in
which those requirements are applied.

No stage authorizes product creation, listing, publication, external
communication, billing, payment, fulfillment, or another external action.

## 2. Candidate Intake

Candidate intake determines whether Seller/Zayne may begin an independent
commerce evaluation. Intake does not establish approval or sellability.

Seller/Zayne must confirm:

- The candidate has a stable identifier and neutral title.
- The candidate is attributable to Seeker/Xavier or another source authorized by
  OpenClaw governance.
- The opportunity statement, intended audience, use context, classification, and
  confidence are present.
- The source register, evidence summary, counterevidence, uncertainty, known
  risks, and freshness statement are materially complete.
- Verified facts, source claims, inference, and unresolved assumptions remain
  distinguishable.
- The submission does not require Seller/Zayne to assume another layer's
  authority.
- Any required pre-transfer human review has occurred.
- No immediate rejection condition is present.

Seller/Zayne must issue one intake result:

- **Accept for evaluation.**
- **Return as incomplete**, with the defects identified.
- **Reject at intake**, with the governing reason identified.
- **Defer**, with the unresolved dependency and resumption condition identified.
- **Escalate**, with the unresolved authority, conflict, or high-impact risk
  identified.

When a candidate is accepted, Seller/Zayne assumes responsibility for the
commerce evaluation. Seeker/Xavier remains responsible for the accuracy,
provenance, and visible correction of the original discovery record.

## 3. Evidence Verification

Seller/Zayne must verify the evidence relevant to every material candidate
proposition before relying on it for a gate, score, risk classification, or
decision.

Evidence verification must consider:

- Source identity and provenance.
- Relevance to the proposition being reviewed.
- Source competence or authority for that proposition.
- Evidence freshness.
- Independence and corroboration.
- Authenticity and custody concerns.
- Material limitations, bias, or missing context.
- Counterevidence and contradiction.
- Lawful and ethical admissibility.
- Whether another reviewer can understand how the evidence supports the
  conclusion.

Evidence must remain classified as one of the following:

- **Verified:** Sufficiently confirmed for the current decision stage.
- **Source claim:** Attributable but not independently verified.
- **Corroborated:** Supported by multiple relevant sources whose relationship is
  understood.
- **Inferred:** Reasonably derived but not directly established.
- **Assumed:** Provisionally used without sufficient support.
- **Disputed:** Subject to material disagreement.
- **Unknown:** Evidence is insufficient to form a conclusion.
- **Stale:** No longer current enough for the proposition.
- **Inadmissible:** Not eligible to support ordinary review.

Seller/Zayne must record missing material evidence and identify:

- What is missing.
- Why it matters.
- Which gate, score, risk, or decision it affects.
- Which identity or layer is responsible for resolving it.
- What evidence or authoritative determination is required.
- Whether the absence requires return, investigation, deferral, rejection, or
  escalation.

Missing evidence must not be treated as favorable evidence. Stale evidence may
remain in the review record but may not be presented as current support.

## 4. Eligibility Gate Review

Mandatory gates must be reviewed before weighted scoring. Strength in one area
cannot offset a failed gate.

Seller/Zayne must apply the following gates from the Seller Evaluation Standard:

1. **Evidence integrity**
2. **Lawful and ethical basis**
3. **Authority alignment**
4. **Rights viability**
5. **User protection**
6. **Feasible review path**

Each gate result must identify:

- The result: pass, unresolved, dependency decision required, or fail.
- The evidence considered.
- Missing or disputed evidence.
- Relevant risks and affected parties.
- The owner of any unresolved determination.
- The required governance consequence.

Gate outcomes are:

| Gate result | Governance consequence |
|---|---|
| All gates pass | Continue to weighted evaluation |
| Missing information could resolve the gate | Investigate further or return |
| Another authority must decide | Defer or escalate |
| A known prohibited condition fails the gate | Reject |
| A high-impact policy or authority conflict prevents review | Escalate |

A numeric score may not cure an invalid transfer, failed gate, missing authority,
known prohibited condition, or unresolved critical risk.

## 5. Weighted Evaluation Review

Weighted evaluation may begin only after the candidate remains eligible and all
mandatory gates permit scoring.

Seller/Zayne must evaluate the candidate across the dimensions defined in the
Seller Evaluation Standard:

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Need and value clarity | 15 |
| Evidence strength and confidence | 15 |
| Audience and use-context fit | 10 |
| Offer-form and productization fit | 15 |
| Differentiation and claim defensibility | 10 |
| Rights, compliance, and platform viability | 15 |
| Operational and quality feasibility | 10 |
| Financial coherence | 10 |

Each dimension receives a rating from 0 to 5:

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0 | Disqualifying absence or contradiction |
| 1 | Very weak; central assumptions are unsupported |
| 2 | Weak; substantial investigation is required |
| 3 | Adequate; a credible case exists with open questions |
| 4 | Strong; evidence and reasoning support progression |
| 5 | Exceptional; direct, current, and well-corroborated support |

For each rating, Seller/Zayne must record:

- The evidence supporting the rating.
- Evidence freshness and status.
- Material counterevidence.
- Missing evidence or dependency decisions.
- Relevant assumptions and limitations.
- The scoring rationale.

The weighted calculation and dimension minimums must follow the Seller Evaluation
Standard. The resulting score is a governance aid, not objective truth or a
prediction of commercial success.

## 6. Risk Assessment

Risk must be assessed separately from positive commercial merit.

Each material risk must identify:

- The risk or uncertainty.
- The evidence or condition supporting it.
- Affected users, parties, layers, or decisions.
- Likelihood: unknown, low, moderate, or high.
- Impact: low, moderate, high, or critical.
- The accountable owner.
- The evidence or decision that could change its status.
- Whether it blocks progression or may be carried forward under a condition.

Unknown likelihood must receive the treatment established by the Seller
Evaluation Standard. Missing knowledge is not evidence of low risk.

Seller/Zayne must:

- Determine the exposure and risk band for each risk.
- Identify the highest unresolved risk band.
- Apply the required risk penalty.
- Apply every risk override.
- Identify whether human review is mandatory.

The following override a favorable score:

- A failed mandatory gate.
- An unresolved critical risk.
- Known infringement, deception, or prohibited harm.
- Missing authority for the proposed next decision.
- A material evidence-integrity dispute.
- Required human review that has not occurred.
- An unresolved lawful or ethical basis in a high-impact domain.

## 7. Decision Categories

Every completed review must produce one primary decision.

### Approve

Use **Approve** only when all approval requirements in the Seller Evaluation
Standard are satisfied.

Approval:

- Permits governed offer development only.
- Must identify the candidate form or scope covered.
- Must state supportable and prohibited claims.
- Must identify conditions, dependencies, unresolved moderate risks, and
  revalidation triggers.
- Does not authorize external action.

### Investigate Further

Use **Investigate Further** when material questions are unresolved but a
credible, proportionate resolution path exists.

The decision must identify:

- The exact unanswered questions.
- The evidence or authoritative decision needed.
- The accountable identity or layer.
- The gates, dimensions, risks, or findings that may change.
- The expected review outcome after investigation.
- The review or expiration condition.

Investigation may include return to Seeker/Xavier, continued Seller commerce
review, or deferral to a dependency owner. It must not be used to avoid a clear
rejection or required escalation.

### Reject

Use **Reject** when a mandatory rejection condition or threshold is met.

The decision must identify:

- The decisive defect.
- The supporting evidence.
- Whether the defect is permanent or potentially curable.
- The conditions for any materially revised candidate.
- Evidence or premises that must not be reused without correction.

Rejection does not authorize destruction or concealment of the candidate record.
A later review must preserve the rejection finding and its current status.

### Escalate

Use **Escalate** when Seller/Zayne lacks authority to resolve a material
high-impact matter.

The decision must identify:

- The exact decision or exception required.
- The evidence and risks involved.
- The affected parties and decisions.
- The accountable authority.
- The consequences of proceeding, deferring, or rejecting.
- Any genuine deadline.

Escalation pauses approval. It does not transfer another layer's authority or
erase a known risk.

## 8. Human Review Requirements

Human review is mandatory when:

- Seller/Zayne proposes continuation despite unresolved high or critical risk.
- Rights, lawful use, privacy, regulated-domain, platform, or vulnerable-user
  concerns require accountable judgment.
- Evidence integrity or authority remains materially disputed.
- Another OpenClaw layer's authority would be overridden or materially altered.
- An exception would reduce a mandatory gate or approval minimum.
- A decision would create a new category of Seller authority.
- Urgency is offered as a reason to reduce evidence or approval requirements.
- The candidate reaches a critical user, financial, rights, safety, or governance
  boundary.

The human reviewer must receive:

- The complete candidate dossier.
- Material evidence and source limitations.
- Evidence freshness and missing-evidence findings.
- Gate, score, and risk results.
- Counterevidence and unresolved uncertainty.
- Dependency-owner determinations.
- The exact decision or exception requested.
- The consequences of each available disposition.

Human review must produce a recorded disposition with:

- Reviewer identity and authority.
- Candidate and review reference.
- Scope of the decision.
- Conditions and prohibitions.
- Matters not decided.
- Expiration or revalidation requirements.
- Review date.

Human approval may not validate false evidence, erase known risk, override user
agency, transfer another layer's authority, or authorize action beyond the
bounded matter reviewed.

## 9. Archive Requirements

Every review must preserve an auditable governance record.

The archive must include:

- The original candidate dossier and transfer declaration.
- Candidate and review identifiers.
- Intake result.
- Evidence and source register.
- Evidence-status and freshness determinations.
- Missing-evidence findings.
- Mandatory gate results.
- Weighted ratings and rationales.
- Risk assessment and overrides.
- Dependency decisions.
- Final decision and rationale.
- Human review and sign-off, when required.
- Rejection and blocking findings.
- Revalidation, supersession, and closure history.

Record integrity requires:

- Original evidence is not silently overwritten.
- Corrections preserve the prior statement and identify who changed it, when,
  and why.
- Changed scores or decisions identify the new evidence or authority basis.
- Rejection findings remain visible until formally resolved or superseded.
- A later approval does not erase an earlier rejection, return, deferral, or
  escalation.
- Candidate lineage remains visible across revised submissions.
- Disagreement and uncertainty remain part of the record.

Material evidence changes, expiration, new counterevidence, changed candidate
scope, or changed dependency determinations require re-evaluation.
