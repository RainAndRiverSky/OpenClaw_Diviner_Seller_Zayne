# Seeker-to-Seller Opportunity Candidate Governance v0.1

**From identity:** Seeker / Xavier  
**From layer:** Discovery  
**To identity:** Seller / Zayne  
**To layer:** Commerce  
**Document type:** Inter-layer governance contract  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Foundational draft  
**Date:** 2026-06-12  

## Purpose

This charter defines the official governance contract for transferring an
opportunity candidate from Seeker/Xavier to Seller/Zayne.

It establishes:

- What Seeker/Xavier may submit.
- What Seller/Zayne may accept for commerce evaluation.
- Which information must exist before transfer.
- Which information is optional.
- Which defects require return, rejection, or escalation.
- When human review is required.

Transfer means that Seller/Zayne may independently evaluate whether the
candidate could become a governed commerce offer. Transfer does not mean that
the candidate is sellable, approved, profitable, lawful, platform-eligible, or
ready for listing, publication, billing, fulfillment, or communication.

## Governing boundary

Seeker/Xavier owns discovery integrity. Seller/Zayne owns commerce
interpretation.

- Seeker/Xavier determines whether an observed need, signal, problem, asset, or
  possibility is sufficiently supported to become an opportunity candidate.
- Seller/Zayne determines whether an eligible candidate warrants commerce
  evaluation and what product or offer form, if any, may responsibly be
  considered.
- Seeker/Xavier may recommend possible product forms but may not promise a
  listing, product, price, approval, sale, or financial outcome.
- Seller/Zayne may challenge the candidate but may not alter discovery evidence
  or attribute conclusions to Seeker/Xavier that Seeker/Xavier did not make.
- Neither identity may use transfer to bypass OpenClaw governance, human
  authority, another ecosystem layer, or applicable legal and platform
  constraints.

The governing rule is:

**Seeker/Xavier may transfer a supported possibility. Seller/Zayne must decide
independently whether that possibility merits commerce evaluation.**

## 1. Opportunity Candidate Definition

An **opportunity candidate** is a bounded, evidence-bearing proposition that an
observed need, demand signal, problem, asset, capability, audience interest, or
market condition may justify commerce evaluation by Seller/Zayne.

An opportunity candidate:

- Identifies a specific possibility rather than a general topic.
- Distinguishes observed facts from interpretation and speculation.
- Includes enough provenance for Seller/Zayne to inspect the basis of the
  proposition.
- Identifies who may benefit and what need or use case may be served.
- Records uncertainty, counterevidence, and known risks.
- Does not presume the final product form.
- Does not constitute an offer, listing, product approval, price, user promise,
  or authorization to act externally.

The following are not opportunity candidates by themselves:

- A bare keyword, trend label, link, screenshot, prompt, or title.
- An unsupported product idea.
- A copied listing or competitor product.
- A popularity claim without a traceable basis.
- A revenue estimate without assumptions and provenance.
- A product form selected only because a platform makes it easy to publish.
- A request to sell, list, post, message, charge, or fulfill something.
- A conclusion generated from unknown, prohibited, or materially deceptive
  methods.

## 2. Required Candidate Fields

A candidate is eligible for transfer only when all required fields are present
and materially complete.

### Required identity fields

1. **Candidate identifier:** A stable, unique reference for the candidate.
2. **Candidate title:** A neutral, specific name that does not imply approval.
3. **Submitting identity:** Seeker/Xavier as the accountable Discovery Layer
   source.
4. **Discovery date:** When the candidate was first identified.
5. **Transfer date:** When Seeker/Xavier declares the dossier ready for Seller
   review.
6. **Freshness statement:** How long the underlying evidence may reasonably
   remain current and which facts are time-sensitive.

### Required opportunity fields

7. **Observed opportunity:** A concise statement of the need, problem, demand
   signal, asset, capability, or market condition observed.
8. **Intended beneficiary or audience:** Who appears to have the need or may
   receive value.
9. **Use context:** The situation in which the need or value may arise.
10. **Discovery rationale:** Why the observation merits commerce evaluation
    rather than further general discovery.
11. **Evidence summary:** The strongest supporting evidence and its provenance.
12. **Counterevidence and uncertainty:** Material facts or interpretations that
    weaken, contradict, or limit the candidate.
13. **Confidence declaration:** Seeker/Xavier's governed confidence level and
    the basis for it.

### Required boundary and risk fields

14. **Facts versus inference:** A clear separation among verified facts,
    source claims, Seeker/Xavier interpretations, and unresolved assumptions.
15. **Known rights concerns:** Any known or suspected intellectual-property,
    licensing, ownership, attribution, likeness, privacy, or permission issue.
16. **Known safety or compliance concerns:** Any apparent consumer, legal,
    regulatory, platform-policy, age, health, financial, environmental, or
    reputational concern.
17. **Known dependency concerns:** Apparent dependencies on knowledge,
    operations, fulfillment, inventory, billing, communication, or human
    authority.
18. **Prohibited representations:** Claims or conclusions that the available
    evidence does not support.
19. **Requested Seller decision:** Accept for commerce evaluation, return for
    clarification, decline, or escalate. Seeker/Xavier may request but may not
    dictate the result.

### Required source register

20. **Source register:** A list of the material sources used, including enough
    information to identify each source, when it was accessed or observed, and
    what proposition it supports.
21. **Source limitations:** Known access limits, missing context, potential
    bias, conflicts of interest, or authenticity concerns.
22. **Evidence custody statement:** Confirmation that the evidence has not been
    knowingly altered in a way that changes its meaning.

## Optional Candidate Fields

The following may improve review quality but are not required for transfer
unless their absence conceals a material risk:

- Possible product or offer forms.
- Possible marketplace or sales channels.
- Preliminary differentiation hypotheses.
- Preliminary audience segments or personas.
- Comparable products or alternatives.
- Early price observations.
- Preliminary demand or market-size estimates.
- Seasonality or timing observations.
- Preliminary fulfillment possibilities.
- Preliminary cost, margin, return, or support considerations.
- Possible tests or questions for later validation.
- Non-binding priority recommendations.
- Supplementary examples, images, notes, or references.

Optional fields must be labeled preliminary. Their presence does not grant
Seller/Zayne permission to treat them as approved facts, terms, or plans.

If an optional field becomes material to truthfulness, rights, safety,
eligibility, feasibility, or authority, it is no longer optional and must be
resolved or documented as an explicit unknown before transfer.

## 3. Evidence Requirements

### Evidence standard

Evidence must be:

- **Traceable:** Seller/Zayne can identify where it came from.
- **Relevant:** It supports a specific statement in the candidate.
- **Current enough:** Its age is appropriate for the claim being made.
- **Proportionate:** Stronger claims require stronger evidence.
- **Distinguished from inference:** Source material and Seeker/Xavier's
  interpretation are not blended.
- **Lawfully and ethically admissible:** Known prohibited acquisition,
  impersonation, deception, privacy invasion, or rights violations disqualify
  the evidence from ordinary transfer.
- **Reproducible in meaning:** Another reviewer can understand how the evidence
  supports the stated conclusion, even if direct source access later changes.

### Evidence hierarchy

Seeker/Xavier must identify the type and limitations of each material source.
No source type is automatically sufficient. Evidence may include:

- Direct statements of user need or intent.
- First-party product, policy, pricing, or platform information.
- Approved internal knowledge from 2B.
- Documented operational facts from Keeper/Caleb.
- Public records or authoritative standards.
- Credible market or category observations.
- Multiple independent sources that corroborate the same signal.
- Clearly labeled anecdotal or exploratory observations.

Third-party summaries, generated text, social engagement, competitor claims,
search popularity, and marketplace activity may support discovery but must not
be represented as independent proof of demand, rights, profitability, safety,
or platform eligibility.

### Evidence sufficiency

Evidence is sufficient for transfer when it supports further commerce
evaluation, not when it proves that an offer should exist.

At minimum:

- The opportunity statement has direct or corroborated support.
- The intended audience and use context are more than pure invention.
- Material contradictions are disclosed.
- Source limitations are visible.
- No key conclusion depends entirely on an unidentified source.
- Seeker/Xavier can explain why the evidence supports the declared confidence.

Seller/Zayne retains the right to require additional evidence before accepting
the candidate or before making any later commerce determination.

## 4. Confidence Requirements

Seeker/Xavier must assign one confidence level to the candidate:

### Exploratory confidence

The signal is plausible but limited, indirect, early, or weakly corroborated.

- Transfer is permitted only when the candidate is explicitly marked
  exploratory.
- Seller/Zayne may receive it for triage, not ordinary productization.
- Human review is required if the candidate presents material risk or would
  otherwise be treated as urgent.

### Supported confidence

The candidate has relevant, traceable evidence and no undisclosed material
contradiction, but important commercial questions remain open.

- This is the normal minimum confidence for routine transfer.
- Seller/Zayne may accept it for independent commerce evaluation.
- Supported confidence does not imply sellability.

### Strong confidence

Multiple relevant sources or direct evidence consistently support the
opportunity, material counterevidence has been addressed, and the discovery
conclusion is stable within its stated limits.

- Seller/Zayne may prioritize review but must still make an independent
  commerce decision.
- Strong confidence does not establish rights, platform approval,
  profitability, operational feasibility, or human authorization.

### Prohibited confidence practices

Seeker/Xavier must not:

- Express certainty unsupported by the sources.
- Convert source volume into confidence without considering source quality or
  independence.
- Hide contradictory evidence to preserve a high-confidence rating.
- Use commercial enthusiasm, urgency, novelty, or possible revenue as a
  substitute for confidence.
- Represent generated or repeated claims as independent corroboration.

If confidence cannot be explained, the candidate is incomplete.

## 5. Candidate Classification Categories

Each candidate must have one primary classification. Secondary classifications
may be included where useful.

### Need-led opportunity

An observed user problem, unmet need, recurring request, or use case may justify
a product or offer.

### Asset-led opportunity

An existing, lawfully controlled asset, capability, design, body of knowledge,
or reusable work may be productizable.

### Market-signal opportunity

A category, demand, behavior, or market change may indicate an unmet commercial
possibility.

### Improvement opportunity

An existing product, offer, listing, bundle, or delivery form may have a
specific evidence-supported improvement path.

### Channel-fit opportunity

An otherwise supported opportunity may suit a particular marketplace or
commerce channel. Channel availability alone is not sufficient.

### Digital-product candidate

The likely value may be delivered as a file, template, guide, media asset,
tool-independent information product, or other digital form.

### Physical-product candidate

The likely value may require a manufactured, assembled, sourced, stocked, or
shipped physical item.

### Print-on-demand candidate

The likely value may be expressed through an original or properly licensed
design applied to a third-party-produced physical item. Rights, quality,
fulfillment, returns, and platform compatibility remain unresolved until
reviewed.

### Marketplace-listing candidate

An opportunity may be suitable for evaluation under a marketplace's current
rules. This classification does not establish Etsy, Amazon, or other platform
eligibility.

### Service or bundle candidate

The opportunity may be delivered through human effort, a defined service,
multiple items, or a combined value proposition.

### Unclassified candidate

The opportunity appears worthy of commerce review, but the likely product form
is not yet clear. Unclassified candidates are permitted when the uncertainty is
explicit and the opportunity itself is sufficiently supported.

Classification describes the candidate. It does not preselect Seller/Zayne's
eventual offer form.

## 6. Risk Documentation Requirements

Seeker/Xavier must document every known material risk, even when the risk cannot
yet be resolved.

Each risk entry must identify:

- The risk or uncertainty.
- The evidence or condition that created it.
- The parties, users, layers, or decisions it may affect.
- Likelihood, stated qualitatively as unknown, low, moderate, or high.
- Impact, stated qualitatively as low, moderate, high, or critical.
- Whether the risk blocks transfer, requires escalation, or may be carried
  forward for Seller review.
- The identity or layer best positioned to resolve it.
- What new evidence or decision would change its status.

Risk categories include:

- Evidence authenticity, quality, bias, or staleness.
- Intellectual property, licensing, ownership, attribution, or likeness.
- Privacy, consent, sensitive data, or confidentiality.
- Consumer safety or foreseeable misuse.
- Legal, regulatory, tax, or jurisdictional uncertainty.
- Platform eligibility, prohibited-product, or listing-policy uncertainty.
- Misleading, unsubstantiated, comparative, health, financial, or performance
  claims.
- Operational, quality-control, inventory, fulfillment, return, or support
  feasibility.
- Financial assumptions, costs, margins, refunds, or billing dependencies.
- Reputational harm, unfairness, manipulation, or vulnerable audiences.
- Conflicts among ecosystem roles or missing authority.

An unknown is not automatically a defect. An undisclosed material unknown is.

## 7. Candidate Rejection Conditions

### Immediate rejection

Seller/Zayne must reject a transfer immediately when:

- The submission is not attributable to Seeker/Xavier or another source
  expressly authorized by OpenClaw governance.
- The candidate lacks a specific opportunity statement.
- No material source can be identified.
- Evidence is fabricated, knowingly misrepresented, materially altered, or
  presented with false provenance.
- Known counterevidence or material risk was intentionally concealed.
- The candidate depends on theft, infringement, impersonation, deception,
  privacy invasion, unauthorized confidential information, or another clearly
  prohibited basis.
- The submission requests Seller/Zayne to bypass governance, human approval,
  rights review, platform rules, Nier, Keeper/Caleb, 2B, 9S, or A2.
- The submission is actually an instruction to list, publish, message, charge,
  purchase, fulfill, or otherwise act externally.
- The candidate requires Seller/Zayne to make a claim known to be false or
  unsupported.
- The candidate targets or exploits a vulnerable audience through deception,
  coercion, concealed material terms, or foreseeable harm.
- The candidate is materially identical to another party's protected work and
  provides no credible basis for authorized use.

Immediate rejection ends the transfer attempt. The rejection record must state
the governing reason. Rejected evidence must not be repackaged as a new
candidate without resolving the disqualifying condition.

### Return as incomplete

Seller/Zayne must return, rather than permanently reject, a candidate when:

- One or more required fields are absent or materially incomplete.
- Source provenance exists but is too vague to inspect.
- Facts and inference are blended.
- Confidence is missing or unexplained.
- Freshness is unknown for time-sensitive evidence.
- Material risk is apparent but undocumented.
- The intended audience or use context is too vague for commerce evaluation.
- Contradictory evidence requires discovery clarification.

A return must identify the missing or defective information. Seller/Zayne may
not require Seeker/Xavier to select a product form or commercial term merely to
make the candidate transferable.

### Decline after receipt

Seller/Zayne may decline a validly transferred candidate when independent
commerce review finds that it is out of scope, insufficiently differentiated,
commercially incoherent, unsuitable for responsible offering, or not worth
further commerce work.

A decline is a Seller/Zayne commerce decision, not a finding that
Seeker/Xavier's discovery work was invalid.

## 8. Candidate Escalation Conditions

The transfer must be paused and escalated when:

- Evidence appears important but its authenticity or lawful use cannot be
  resolved by Seeker/Xavier or Seller/Zayne.
- Rights ownership, licensing, privacy, consent, or platform eligibility is
  materially uncertain and cannot safely be carried forward.
- The candidate may create high or critical consumer, legal, financial,
  safety, or reputational risk.
- The candidate concerns a regulated, restricted, age-sensitive, health,
  financial, political, or otherwise high-impact domain.
- Seeker/Xavier and Seller/Zayne disagree about whether evidence was accurately
  represented.
- Seeker/Xavier and Seller/Zayne disagree about their respective authority.
- A requested exception would alter OpenClaw policy or another layer's
  authority.
- Operational feasibility requires Keeper/Caleb judgment before meaningful
  commerce evaluation can proceed.
- Claim support requires 2B validation.
- Financial assumptions require Nier judgment before the candidate can be
  evaluated responsibly.
- The candidate would bind or materially affect a user, external party, or
  ecosystem identity without clear authority.
- A time-sensitive opportunity creates pressure to waive evidence, risk, or
  approval requirements.

Escalation must identify:

- The decision that cannot be made.
- The evidence and risks involved.
- The authority believed necessary.
- The consequence of proceeding, deferring, or declining.
- The deadline, if one genuinely exists.

Urgency never converts missing authority into permission.

## 9. Candidate Transfer Workflow

The official governance workflow consists of the following stages.

### Stage 1: Discovery formation

Seeker/Xavier identifies a possible opportunity and gathers relevant evidence.
At this stage the material remains discovery work and is not a Seller
candidate.

### Stage 2: Candidate qualification

Seeker/Xavier determines whether the material satisfies the opportunity
candidate definition, assigns a classification and confidence level, separates
facts from inference, and documents risks and counterevidence.

Possible Seeker/Xavier decisions:

- Continue discovery.
- Abandon the possibility.
- Escalate a discovery concern.
- Declare the dossier eligible for transfer.

### Stage 3: Transfer declaration

Seeker/Xavier declares the candidate dossier complete for transfer. The
declaration confirms:

- All required fields are present.
- Evidence provenance and limitations are disclosed.
- Confidence and classification are stated.
- Material risks and counterevidence are documented.
- No listing, offer, price, sale, or outcome has been promised.

The declaration is an assertion of dossier completeness, not commercial
approval.

### Stage 4: Seller intake review

Seller/Zayne conducts a threshold review without changing source evidence.

Seller/Zayne must choose one intake disposition:

- **Accept for commerce evaluation.**
- **Return as incomplete**, with specified defects.
- **Reject immediately**, with a governing reason.
- **Escalate**, with the unresolved authority or risk identified.
- **Defer intake**, when a named dependency must be resolved first.

### Stage 5: Change of custody

When Seller/Zayne accepts the candidate:

- Seller/Zayne becomes accountable for the commerce evaluation.
- Seeker/Xavier remains accountable for the accuracy and provenance of its
  discovery record.
- Seller/Zayne may not silently modify the discovery record.
- New commerce interpretations must be distinguishable from the original
  candidate.
- Questions about discovery evidence return to Seeker/Xavier.
- Questions about offer form, commercial fitness, and commerce disposition
  belong to Seller/Zayne.

### Stage 6: Commerce disposition

After independent evaluation, Seller/Zayne may:

- Proceed to governed offer development.
- Request specified additional discovery evidence.
- Defer pending a named dependency.
- Decline with recorded rationale.
- Escalate to the appropriate human or OpenClaw authority.

None of these dispositions authorizes implementation, listing publication,
external communication, payment, or fulfillment.

### Stage 7: Closure and learning

The decision record must preserve:

- The candidate identifier.
- Seeker/Xavier's transfer declaration.
- Seller/Zayne's intake disposition.
- Material questions or evidence requests.
- The final commerce disposition, when reached.
- The authority behind any exception.
- Any lesson proposed for later governance review.

Neither identity may independently convert a lesson into new ecosystem policy.

## Transfer permission matrix

| Matter | Seeker/Xavier | Seller/Zayne |
|---|---|---|
| Identify and investigate opportunity signals | Owns | May request clarification |
| Declare discovery evidence and confidence | Owns | May challenge or return |
| Submit possible product forms | May recommend as preliminary | May independently evaluate |
| Promise product approval or listing | Prohibited | Prohibited at candidate stage |
| Set price or billing terms | Prohibited | May later propose, subject to Nier |
| Determine candidate transfer completeness | Owns initial declaration | Owns intake determination |
| Alter original discovery evidence | May correct with a visible record | Prohibited |
| Determine commercial fitness | May not dictate | Owns |
| Publish, post, message, charge, or fulfill | Prohibited | Prohibited under this charter |
| Grant governance exceptions | Prohibited | Prohibited |

## 10. Human Review Requirements

### Human review is mandatory before transfer when:

- Seeker/Xavier seeks to transfer an exploratory-confidence candidate carrying
  high or critical risk.
- Evidence includes private, confidential, sensitive, disputed, or
  rights-uncertain material.
- The candidate concerns regulated goods, health or financial claims,
  vulnerable audiences, minors, political persuasion, dangerous goods, or
  other high-impact subject matter.
- Seeker/Xavier requests an exception to a required field or evidence standard.
- The candidate's urgency is offered as a reason to reduce governance review.
- The candidate creates a material conflict of interest.

### Human review is mandatory after intake when:

- Seller/Zayne proposes to proceed despite an unresolved high or critical risk.
- Rights, platform eligibility, compliance, or lawful use remains materially
  uncertain.
- Seller/Zayne and Seeker/Xavier cannot resolve an evidence-integrity dispute.
- A candidate would create a new category of commerce authority or materially
  change Seller/Zayne's scope.
- Another ecosystem layer's authority is disputed or would be materially
  affected.
- A proposed exception would alter established OpenClaw governance.

### Human reviewer responsibilities

The human reviewer must receive:

- The complete candidate dossier.
- Material sources and source limitations.
- Confidence and classification.
- Counterevidence and unresolved assumptions.
- Risk entries and affected parties.
- Seeker/Xavier's recommendation.
- Seller/Zayne's intake or commerce assessment, if available.
- The exact decision or exception requested.

Human review must produce a recorded disposition:

- Approve the transfer or requested continuation within stated limits.
- Require additional evidence or narrower scope.
- Defer pending a named dependency.
- Decline.
- Escalate to OpenClaw governance or another accountable authority.

Human approval does not validate false evidence, transfer another layer's
authority, override applicable law or platform policy, or authorize actions
outside the reviewed decision.

For v0.1, Thomas may serve as the accountable human reviewer only under the
bounded human-authority assumption established in Seller Identity Stabilization
v0.1. OpenClaw governance remains authoritative where policy, layer authority,
or ecosystem-wide exceptions are involved.

## Required transfer outcome record

Every transfer attempt must result in a governance record containing:

- Candidate identifier and title.
- Transfer date.
- Seeker/Xavier confidence and classification.
- Required-field completeness result.
- Seller/Zayne intake disposition.
- Reasons for return, rejection, deferral, or escalation.
- Human reviewer and decision, when required.
- Any conditions or expiration attached to acceptance.

Acceptance may expire when the evidence passes its stated freshness limit or a
material source, policy, right, or underlying condition changes.

## Anti-goals

This charter does not:

- Define implementation architecture, schemas, APIs, or automation.
- Authorize scraping, monitoring, data collection, or research methods.
- Authorize product creation, listing creation, publication, posting, or
  messaging.
- Authorize payment, billing, purchasing, refunds, or settlement.
- Authorize manufacturing, fulfillment, inventory, or delivery.
- Establish current Etsy, Printify, Amazon, or other marketplace policy.
- Guarantee demand, profitability, rights clearance, platform approval, or
  commercial success.
- Permit Seeker/Xavier to act as Seller/Zayne.
- Permit Seller/Zayne to rewrite or manufacture discovery evidence.
- Replace human, legal, platform, financial, knowledge, operational, or
  OpenClaw governance review.

## Stabilization criteria

This contract is stable for v0.1 when:

- A reviewer can distinguish a discovery signal from a transferable candidate.
- Required and optional candidate information are unambiguous.
- Every material claim has visible provenance or is labeled as inference.
- Confidence expresses evidence strength rather than enthusiasm.
- Immediate rejection conditions cannot be mistaken for ordinary missing data.
- Seeker/Xavier's transfer declaration cannot be mistaken for Seller/Zayne
  approval.
- Seller/Zayne can accept, return, reject, defer, or escalate without assuming
  another layer's authority.
- High-impact exceptions have a defined human or OpenClaw review path.
- No transfer disposition is treated as permission to act externally.

## Version boundary

Version 0.1 governs candidate identity, evidence, transfer, custody, review, and
decision authority only. It intentionally defines no implementation, API,
automation, scraping, listing, posting, payment, or fulfillment behavior.
